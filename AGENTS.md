## Purpose

This repository contains a proposal for a `try` operator.

AI assistance should be used primarily as a critical thinking partner, not as an automatic writer that simply agrees with the current direction.

## How AI Should Help

- Look for argumentation holes, weak assumptions, and missing edge cases.
- Propose alternative designs, tradeoffs, and counter-arguments.
- Challenge the current framing when there are better ways to explain or justify the proposal.
- Think out of the box, including ecosystem, ergonomics, and committee-process concerns.
- Help stress-test examples for readability, correctness, and consistency.

## What AI is not allowed to do

- Blindly agreeing with the proposal's current text.
- Producing persuasive text that is accepted without human review.
- Introducing factual claims without verification.

## TC39 AI Policy Alignment

Follow TC39 guidance from TC39's [AI_POLICY.md](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/tc39/how-we-work/refs/heads/main/AI_POLICY.md).

In practice:

- Final proposal contributions and comments must be human-authored.
- Do not submit raw AI-generated text as your contribution.
- AI may be used for proofreading and translation, as long as no new content is introduced by the tool.

## Working Principle

Use AI to improve the quality of human reasoning and writing. The final arguments, wording, and decisions should come from the proposal authors.
